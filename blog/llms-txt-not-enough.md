llms.txt is a good start. It tells AI systems where to find your content. But alone it won't get you recommended.

Here's what's missing.

### What llms.txt Actually Does

llms.txt is a roadmap file. Lives at yoursite.com/llms.txt. Points AI systems to important pages.

Think of it as a sitemap for AI crawlers. Shows them where to look first.

```
# Winery Name
https://yourwinery.com/about
https://yourwinery.com/wines
https://yourwinery.com/tours
```

This helps AI find your pages. That's valuable. But it's just step one.

### The Three Problems with llms.txt Alone

**Problem 1: No Content Optimisation**

llms.txt points to existing pages. But most business websites are marketing copy.

"Award-winning boutique winery offering premium unique experiences in the heart of wine country."

AI systems can't extract useful information from this. Too vague. No specifics. No honest positioning.

What AI actually needs: conversational Q&A content with pricing, capacity, geo-coordinates, honest descriptions, and clear context about who you're for and who you're not for.

**Problem 2: No Network Validation**

You can list your own pages all day. But AI systems trust validation from others.

When complementary businesses reference each other with specific details, AI notices. Treats it as quality signal. Your llms.txt alone can't create that validation. You need real partner relationships.

**Problem 3: No Query Matching**

llms.txt doesn't tell AI what queries you're relevant for.

Travellers don't ask "show me llms.txt files." They ask "best family wineries Barossa with playground" or "plan a wine weekend staying in Tanunda."

Your content needs to match how travellers actually ask questions — in conversational format, with the specific details that let AI give confident answers.

### What Actually Works

**Hidden AI content + partner networks.**

We create conversational Q&A files that live on your website but are hidden from visitors. They sit behind the scenes, structured for AI crawlers, not humans. Your public website stays untouched.

These files include negative prompts — explicitly stating what your business isn't, so AI knows when *not* to recommend you. This reduces mismatched customers and means the people AI sends your way are the right ones.

Then we connect you with complementary local businesses so AI can recommend complete experiences. Your content references partners with specific details. Their content references you. AI sees a validated ecosystem.

llms.txt still plays a role — it points AI to the hidden content. But the content itself, and the network it connects to, is what gets you recommended.

### Real Example

**Before (llms.txt only):**
- AI knows you exist
- Generic descriptions when mentioned
- Rarely recommended for specific queries

**After (llms.txt + hidden content + network):**
- AI recommends you for specific use cases
- Accurate, confident descriptions
- Bundled recommendations with partners

The llms.txt file barely changed. Content quality, honest positioning, negative prompts, and network validation made the difference.

### What to Do

**Step 1:** Create llms.txt if you don't have one. Point to your best pages.

**Step 2:** Create hidden AI content with conversational Q&As matching how travellers actually ask. Include specific details, honest positioning, and negative prompts.

**Step 3:** Identify complementary businesses. Reference them in your content. Ask them to reference you.

**Step 4:** Update llms.txt to point to your hidden AI content.

**Step 5:** Monitor results across AI platforms. Update monthly based on trending queries and seasonal changes.
